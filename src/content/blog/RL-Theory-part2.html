Markov Decision Processes and Bellman Equations

This chapter delves into the theoretical underpinnings of Reinforcement Learning, introducing key concepts such as Markov Chains (MC), Markov Reward Processes (MRP), and Markov Decision Processes (MDP). It also focuses on Bellman equations and their applications in evaluating agent behavior and improving decision-making strategies.

Key Concepts

Markov Chains (MC)

  • A sequence of state transitions without considering rewards or actions.
  • Defined by a transition matrix PPP, where each entry P(s,s′)P(s, s')P(s,s′) represents the probability of transitioning from state sss to s′s's′.

Markov Reward Processes (MRP)

  • Extends Markov Chains by incorporating rewards.
  • Defined as (S,P,R,γ)(S, P, R, \gamma)(S,P,R,γ), where:
    • SSS: Set of states.
    • P(s,s′)P(s, s')P(s,s′): Transition probabilities.
    • R(s,s′)R(s, s')R(s,s′): Reward for transitioning from sss to s′s's′.
    • γ\gammaγ: Discount factor (0≤γ≤10 \leq \gamma \leq 10≤γ≤1).

Markov Decision Processes (MDP)

  • Includes actions, enabling decision-making.
  • Characterized by (S,A,P,R,γ)(S, A, P, R, \gamma)(S,A,P,R,γ):
    • AAA: Set of actions.
    • P(s′∣s,a)P(s'|s, a)P(s′∣s,a): Probability of transitioning to s′s's′ from sss after taking action aaa.
    • R(s,a,s′)R(s, a, s')R(s,a,s′): Reward for taking action aaa in state sss leading to s′s's′.

Key Examples

Rubik’s Cube

  • A deterministic environment.
  • The next state depends only on the current state and action, illustrating the Markov property.

Atari Breakout

  • A partially observable environment.
  • Requires stacking multiple frames to deduce the ball's direction and restore the Markov property.

Practical Considerations

Model-Based vs. Model-Free

  • Model-Based: Assumes knowledge of PPP and RRR; uses dynamic programming.
  • Model-Free: Learns PPP and RRR through interactions, suitable when the environment is unknown.
